Molecular Modeling and Simulations
Mini Project: Escaping the Pentane Barrier

1. Project Overview
Molecular simulations often become trapped in local free-energy minima and therefore fail to
explore the full conformational space of a system within practical simulation times. This project
investigates this issue using n-pentane as a model system.
The central backbone of n-pentane exhibits torsional flexibility, and rotation about the
backbone dihedral gives rise to multiple conformational states separated by energy barriers. In
a straightforward simulation, the system may remain confined to a single well for a long time,
thereby providing a clear example of poor sampling.
The aim of this project is to develop a Python-based workflow that first demonstrates this
trapping behavior and then introduces an enhanced sampling strategy that improves barrier
crossing and conformational exploration.
Students must use a united-atom (UA) representation of n-pentane. In a UA model,
hydrogen atoms attached to carbon are not treated as separate interaction sites; instead, they
are incorporated into the carbon centers. This reduces the total number of particles and lowers the computational cost while preserving the essential torsional behavior of the molecule.
The model must be parameterized using the published TraPPE-UA force field. Students
should refer to the original paper at this link and to the parameter database available at
http://trappe.oit.umn.edu/UA.
The workflow must include both Monte Carlo (MC) sampling and NVT molecular
dynamics (MD).

2. Problem Statement
For an n-pentane molecule represented using the TraPPE-UA force field, a naive simulation
may remain trapped in one conformational region because of torsional energy barriers.
Your task is to:
• generate an initial configuration for the molecule,
• demonstrate baseline conformational sampling using MC and NVT MD,
• compare the dynamics at 120 K and 250 K,
• compare the dihedral distributions obtained from MC, MD, and enhanced sampling,
• compute the potential of mean force (PMF) using the Boltzmann inversion formula,
• implement an enhanced sampling method that improves barrier crossing,
• quantify the improvement in conformational exploration.
All implementations must be written in Python.
The use of standard molecular dynamics engines is not permitted. This includes, but is not
limited to:
• GROMACS
• LAMMPS
• AMBER

3. Required Conditions for All Submissions
All submissions must satisfy the following conditions:
• Molecular system: n-pentane
• Representation: united-atom (UA)
• Force field: TraPPE-UA
• Reference paper: TraPPE-UA original paper
• Parameter source: TraPPE-UA parameter database
• Dihedral of interest: backbone dihedral defined by atoms 1–2–3–4
• Temperature: 120 K and 250 K
• MD ensemble: NVT
• Simulation length: 200,000 steps
• Dihedral discretization: 36 equal bins over −180◦ to 180◦
Students must show that the baseline simulation exhibits trapping before applying the enhanced sampling method.

4. Task 1: Initial Configuration Generation

(10 Marks)

Students must generate a physically reasonable initial configuration of n-pentane in the unitedatom representation.
The initial configuration should be prepared in Python and should serve as the starting point
for both the Monte Carlo and NVT MD simulations. The configuration must be consistent with
the chosen TraPPE-UA model and the dihedral definition specified above.

5. Task 2: Baseline Sampling Using MC and NVT MD

(25 Marks)

Students must implement baseline Monte Carlo and NVT MD simulations in Python using
the TraPPE-UA force field.
The purpose of this stage is to demonstrate the limitations of naive sampling. The simulations should be performed at both 120 K and 250 K. Students should compare the dynamics
at the two temperatures and discuss how temperature affects barrier crossing, conformational
transitions, and trapping in local minima. The submitted analysis should include the dihedral
angle time series, a histogram of sampled states, comparison of the dihedral distributions obtained from MC and MD, evidence that barrier crossing is infrequent, and a comparison between
the MC and NVT MD behavior at the two temperatures.

6. Task 3: Enhanced Sampling Method

(40 Marks)

Students must design and implement an enhanced sampling algorithm that improves exploration
of the torsional landscape and enables barrier crossing.
The method must be implemented entirely in Python and integrated into the simulation
workflow. The report should clearly explain the algorithmic design, how it is incorporated into
the simulation, and why it is expected to improve sampling relative to the baseline methods.
The submitted results should demonstrate improved conformational exploration. Students
should also compare the dihedral distributions obtained from the enhanced method against
those from the baseline MC and MD simulations.

Potential of Mean Force
Using the sampled dihedral distribution, students should compute the PMF by applying the
Boltzmann inversion formula,
F (ϕ) = −kB T ln P (ϕ) + C
where P (ϕ) is the normalized dihedral probability distribution, kB is the Boltzmann constant, T is the temperature, and C is an arbitrary constant.
The PMF should be reported for the baseline and enhanced sampling methods, and the
free-energy profile should be discussed in relation to the observed conformational states.
